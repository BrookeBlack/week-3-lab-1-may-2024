I love the Northern Lights!

Aurora Borealis! These beautiful lights are created in a curtain like fashion in the sky! These lights are from a combination of changes in the sky's magnetosphere and change the paths in the Earth's charged particles! There are many factors that go into play, but it creats a brilliant pattern light show for us to see.

Galileo Galilei in 1619 named these amazing light shows, he created the name using refrences to the Roman Aurora, goddess of the dawn, and the Greek Boreas, god of the cold north wind. These light shows are most commonly seen in the "auroral oval", which extended from mid Canada to the mid region of the U.S. However, they can be seen sometimes as far south as the northern parts of the souther U.S. states!

There are many places to read more about these stunning lights online! Your local libary will also have a astounding selection on this topic as well! I will attach some links for reading as well to further expand your knowledge and understanding of the complexities of these beautiful light shows!

Things to know about the Aurora:

  • they are charged particles interacting in the sky
  • they're called "Northern Lights" because they are primarily seen in the northern U.S. states, but are seen in the northern most portion of the northern hemisphere
  • they appear as ribbons or curtsins in the sky
